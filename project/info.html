The Faculty of Science is devoted to excellence in teaching, learning, and research, and to developing leaders in many disciplines who make a difference globally. The faculty, which is based in Hadath, has an enrollment of over 15,000 degree candidates, including undergraduate, graduate, and professional students.

History

One of the first and largest Lebanese University faculties, the Faculty of Sciences was established in 1959. In the 1970s, it was the core around which Rafik Hariri University Campus, the largest Campus of the Lebanese University, was built in Hadath.

About

The Faculty is divided into five branches in five governorates, in addition to the sections in Akkar, Baalbeck, Bint-Jbeil, Byblos (Jbeil), Dibbiyeh and Tyre (Sour). It adopts continuously updated scientific programs to provide the job market with distinguished graduates. It diligently develops knowledge through learning, scientific research laboratories, research teams, and higher education programs.

Faculty at a Glance

The Faculty of Science grants licenses in eleven fields including Mathematics, Physics, Biochemistry, Geology etc. It also offers excellent programs for master studies within the various fields of its teaching.
